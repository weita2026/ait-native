# ait Trademark Policy

Authority: command layer under [docs/plan.md](./plan.md) and [docs/legal_plan.md](./legal_plan.md).  
Status: current trademark and brand-usage policy draft for public launch preparation.  
Revision date: 2026-05-19.  
Scope: define brand ownership, permitted and forbidden mark use, official-domain posture, fork/integration naming rules, and the boundary between source-code licenses and trademark rights for the `ait` product family.

> This file is a trademark/brand-usage policy draft, not legal advice. Final trademark filing, enforcement, and jurisdiction strategy should be reviewed with qualified counsel.

## 1. Official brand architecture

The current public brand architecture is:

- **`ait-native`** — public umbrella brand and official website brand
- **`ait`** — core CLI and local trust-layer product/component mark
- **`ait-agent`** — companion transport/runtime component mark
- **`ait-server`** — shared control-plane component mark
- **`ait-worker`** — shared control-plane worker component mark

Official website domain:

- **`https://ait-native.dev`**

Official brand assets include the relevant wordmarks, logos, icons, and public
site assets the project intentionally publishes as official brand material.

## 2. Licenses do not grant trademark rights

Source-code licenses, documentation licenses, and public package availability
do **not** by themselves grant trademark rights in:

- `ait-native`
- `ait`
- `ait-agent`
- `ait-server`
- `ait-worker`
- related wordmarks, logos, icons, or other official brand assets
- confusingly similar domains, subdomains, handles, or branding

Use the applicable software/content license for code or documentation rights,
and use this policy for trademark/brand-use rules.

## 3. Permitted nominative and community use

Without separate written permission, you may use the marks only as reasonably
necessary to:

- refer factually to the project or its official components;
- describe compatibility, integration, or support truthfully;
- link to the official website at `https://ait-native.dev`;
- discuss, review, benchmark, document, or compare the project; or
- identify an unmodified redistribution that accurately preserves the official
  source, notices, and applicable license terms.

This permission does **not** allow you to imply sponsorship, endorsement,
partnership, certification, or official status where none exists.

## 4. Forbidden use

Without separate written permission, you may **not**:

- use the marks in a way that implies endorsement or official affiliation;
- use confusingly similar product, company, domain, or social-handle names;
- present a fork, hosted service, plugin, package, or integration as the
  official project when it is not;
- alter or combine logos/wordmarks in a misleading way;
- remove trademark notices from official materials where they are present; or
- use the marks in connection with unlawful, deceptive, or harmful conduct.

## 5. Forks and modified distributions

If you publish a fork or modified distribution:

- do not call it the official `ait-native` website or the official `ait`
  project unless it actually is;
- do not use `ait-native.dev` or confusingly similar domains/subdomains;
- make the fork/modification status visible in the name or description; and
- preserve applicable copyright, license, and attribution notices.

Accurate statements such as “forked from ait” or “compatible with ait” are
generally allowed if they are truthful and non-misleading.

## 6. Plugins, integrations, and transport naming

Compatible plugins, integrations, and transport workers may use nominative
references such as:

- “for ait”
- “compatible with ait”
- “integration for ait-native”

They may **not** use names that imply they are the official project itself.

For `ait-agent` third-party transports:

- keep transport/developer naming clear and specific;
- do not imply that an unofficial transport is the canonical official runtime
  unless the project has designated it as such; and
- avoid names or package identifiers likely to confuse developers about whether
  the transport is official, community-maintained, or commercial.

## 7. Domain and handle rules

The official website domain is:

- `https://ait-native.dev`

Without written permission, do not register or use domains, subdomains, social
handles, or package names that are confusingly similar to the official marks in
a way that is likely to mislead users.

Domain control alone does not replace trademark clearance, filing, or
enforcement work.

## 8. Logo and brand-asset usage

Official brand assets may be used only for:

- truthful reference to the project;
- linking to the official project or official website;
- press/review/educational coverage; or
- other uses explicitly allowed in writing.

Do not modify official logos/wordmarks in a way that creates confusion about
source, status, endorsement, or ownership.

## 9. Questions and permissions

If a planned use is commercial, prominent, customer-facing, domain-related, or
otherwise likely to create confusion, treat it as requiring explicit review and
permission rather than assumption.
